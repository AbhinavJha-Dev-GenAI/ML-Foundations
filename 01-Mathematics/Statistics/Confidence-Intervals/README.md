# Confidence Intervals

Estimating the range within which a population parameter is likely to fall.

### 1. Core Definition
A Confidence Interval (CI) gives an estimated range of values which is likely to include an unknown population parameter, the estimated range being calculated from a given set of sample data.
- **Example**: "We are 95% confident that the mean height is between 170cm and 175cm."

### 2. The Relationship with Sample Size
- **Larger sample size** $\rightarrow$ Smaller Standard Error $\rightarrow$ **Narrower CI** (More precise).
- **Higher confidence level** (e.g., 99% vs 95%) $\rightarrow$ **Wider CI**.

### 3. Margin of Error
The amount of error that the researcher is willing to tolerate. It depends on the confidence level and the variability in the data.

In ML, we don't just want a "point estimate" (a single number). We want to know how certain we are. Confidence intervals help us communicate the reliability of our model's predictions to stakeholders.
